Islam was popularized during the time of Prophet Muhammad (saws), or around the early to mid 600s CE. He was the last prophet of Islam, and is highly respected. He is invoked with the Arabic phrase "صَلَّى اللّٰهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ", or "Sallalahu alaihi wasalam", translating to "Peace and blessings of Allah be upon him". He serves as a role model for humanity. Although Muhammad (saws) is highly revered within the Islamic faith, he is not worshiped as God.

We believe that there is no god but Allah (swt), that he has no partners, and that he is all-powerful and all-knowing. A surah (chapter) of the Quran describes him exceptionally, Surah Al Ikhlas (Sincerity)—
In the name of Allah, the entirely merciful, the especially merciful.
Say "He is Allah, who is one",
Allah, the eternal refuge,
He neither begets nor is born,
Nor is there to him any equivalent.
(Translation from Sahih International)

Muslims believe in a second, eternal life, as do most religions. The outcome of the next life is dependent on one's character in this life. It is also dependent on how good of a Muslim he/she was.
